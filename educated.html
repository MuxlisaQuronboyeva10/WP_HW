On Educated

"To admit uncertainty is to admit to weakness, to powerlessness and to believe in yourself despite both. It is a frailty, but in this frailty there is a strength: the conviction to live in your own mind and not in someone else's." "Who writes history?" "To see and experience more truths than those given to me by my father and to use those truths to construct my own mind. I had come to believe that the ability to evaluate many ideas, many histories, many points of view, was at the heart of what it means to self-create." "None but ourselves can free our minds." "The first time I saw King's College, Cambridge, I didn't think I was dreaming, but only because my imagination had never produced anything so grand." I somehow can relate to Tara Westover's memoir "Educated". It is about her story, her search for her own truth. 'What does educated mean to you? How does it differ from being taught?'. These questions were yielded during the discussion of the first half of this book. Not knowing the whole picture, I tried to understand what the author was trying to tell. I somehow managed to form my own answers to those questions. Here are my answers to these questions: Being educated is a choice while being taught is not. One must go through the phase of being taught in order to pursue education.So, we can't deny the importance of being taught. Education is one's own journey of questioning what has been ingrained in himself. At the end, he'll create his own truth, much different from what he used to consider. Maybe it could be vice versa. In either ways, he'll find out or at least will be in this path, the truth. Final note: presence of something which also might be wrong, is better than absence of it. You at least have an option: to either follow it or create your own path.
